COVID-19 Pandemic Impact on Economy

Tsz Fung Wong, You Shan Shen

Image Credit:https://www.claimsjournal.com/

Motivation

The COVID-19 pandemic has caused a devastating loss of life but it has also devastated the nation’s economy. COVID-19 has a huge direct impact on labor supply shock to each sector arising from morbidity, mortality, and lockdown, as well as the indirect impact caused by the interdependencies between sectors in the US economy. Below are some resources that dive into these topics, from foundation and company:

• KFF published an article about how COVID-19 had set back economic progress worldwide by reporting GDP.

• Deloitte company release article focusing on different aspect of consumer spending during COVID-19.

The purpose of this project was to use quantitative data to assess how COVID-19 changes the personal consumption expenditures. Can we see COVID-19 transform the economic structure, and how it changes compared to the recent 10 years?

Data

We used data publicly available from the Bureau of Economic Analysis. More detailed information on the datasets used can be found in the Data tab.

Research Questions

• How do personal consumption expenditures change during COVID-19 compared in each state? Which state changes the most? What is the highest consumption amount during the pandemic?

• Do the top3 industries in personal consumption expenditures comparing After-/Pre-COVID-19 in each state have a huge change? What are the top3 industries percentages for each one in each state?

• How does the GDP change from 2010 to 2021 in different industries? Compare GDP percentage change in different between different years and different industries? What are the top3 GDP states in each industry in a year?

Main results

• Overall, we can see COVID-19 starting to impact the economy in 2020 and not obvious in 2019.

• Compared to 2018, Louisiana, Oklahoma and Wyoming are the top three that have dramatic decrease in total personal consumption in 2020. In 2020, Private, Finance, and Manufacturing are the top3 highest in consumption.

• Overall, in 2019-2020, the economic structure in consumption didn’t change a lot in each state.

• During the COVID-19, mining, entertainment, and accommodation have a huge decrease in GDP percentage change. Also, GDP decrease in 2019 and 2020 but seems to recovery in 2021. In general, California, New York, and Texas are the top3 GDP amount among all the ither states.
